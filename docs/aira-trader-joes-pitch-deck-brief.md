## Part 1: What Trader Joe's Style Looks Like 🛒

Share this section with your freelancer so the deck *feels* like it belongs in the TJ's world.

### Visual & Brand Vibe
- **Playful, not corporate** — Hand-drawn feel, whimsical illustrations, friendly typography. Think “neighborhood grocery” meets “discovery.”
- **Bright, appetizing, inviting** — Strong color, clear product shots. Nothing dull or generic.
- **Varied but coherent** — TJ's packaging is intentionally “all over the place” (different illustrators, styles per product) but always **fun, clever, and quality-focused**.
- **Cali–Hawaiian, relaxed** — Approachable, warm, slightly quirky. Not stiff or luxury-minimal.
- **Story + product together** — They use packaging and copy to tell a story (origin, maker, cause) while still showing the product clearly.

### What to Avoid
- Uniform “corporate brand” look (same template for every slide).
- Cold, tech-startup aesthetic (gradients, generic icons).
- Overly serious or formal tone.
- Cluttered layouts — TJ's is playful but **disciplined**; info is easy to scan.

### Takeaway for the Deck
The deck should feel **warm, story-driven, and visually engaging** — like something a TJ's buyer would recognize as “our kind of brand” while still being professional and easy to read in a meeting.

## Part 3: Pointers to Give Your Freelancer 🎯

Copy or adapt these when you brief the designer.

### Creative direction
1. **Match Trader Joe's energy, not copy them.** Use warm, playful, illustrated touches (e.g. small icons, hand-drawn borders, or a custom illustration of a tote/animal/artisan) so it feels “TJ's aisle” without using their logo or type.
2. **Lead with product and story.** Use the pitch deck images in `docs/` (see “Pitch deck images” below) or your own. Pair with one clear “impact” line (e.g. “Made by women artisans • 20% to animal rescue”).
3. **Keep type and layout clean.** Playful doesn’t mean chaotic. Clear headings, short body copy, plenty of white space so a buyer can scan quickly.
4. **Color:** Warm, natural, inviting (e.g. cotton whites, soft earth tones, one accent — e.g. terracotta or a soft green). Avoid cold blues and grays.
5. **If they use illustration:** Suggest a simple mascot or motif (e.g. artisan hands, a friendly animal, cotton plant) that can repeat on cover, impact slide, and one-pager for consistency.

### Content and messaging
6. **Emphasize “discovery” and “story.”** TJ's shoppers love “find of the day” and “this has a story.” Position Eira as a discoverable, story-driven brand (artisans + cause).
7. **Be ready for tough margins.** TJ's often pays lower wholesale; the deck doesn’t need to spell that out, but the “Pricing & terms” slide should be clear so you can have that conversation.
8. **One slide on “Why now / why us.”** E.g. sustainable cotton, artisan-made, cause-based — trends TJ's buyers care about. One slide max.
9. **No long paragraphs.** Bullets, short sentences, numbers (e.g. “20% to XYZ Foundation,” “X women artisans”).

### Format and deliverables
10. **Decide primary use:** (a) PDF for Trader Joe's Potential Vendor form + email, (b) trade show loop on a screen, or (c) both. If both: ask for 16:9 for screen and a print-safe version (e.g. PDF with bleed) for one-pagers.
11. **Ask for:**  
    - Full pitch deck (PDF).  
    - One-pager / sell-sheet (1–2 pages, same story, handout or leave-behind).  
    - Optional: linesheet (SKUs, prices, specs).
12. **Trader Joe's submission:** They use an online [Potential Vendor Form](https://www.traderjoes.com/home/contact-us/potential-vendor-form). The deck is your leave-behind or attachment; the form is where you officially apply. Make sure the deck has a “contact” slide that matches what you’ll put in the form.

### Reference and research
13. **Send the freelancer:**  
    - This document (including “Deck copy” and “Pitch deck images”).  
    - 3–5 screenshots or links of Trader Joe's product/packaging you like.  
    - Pitch deck images in `docs/` (eira-pitch-tote.png, eira-pitch-toiletry-set.png) and/or your own product and artisan/cause imagery.
14. **Remind them:** TJ's buyers see hundreds of pitches. The deck should answer in 2 minutes: *Who are you? What are you selling? Why is it special? Why Trader Joe's? What’s the ask?*

## Part 4: Trader Joe's Vendor Process (For You, Not the Deck) 📌

- **Official step:** Submit via [Potential Vendor Form](https://www.traderjoes.com/home/contact-us/potential-vendor-form) on traderjoes.com.
- **Before pitching:** Check if TJ's already carries similar totes or toiletry sets in your price range; if yes, differentiation (story, cause, design) must be very clear.
- **Margins:** Wholesale to TJ's is often lower than typical retail; ensure your numbers work at their expected price points.
- **Category:** Your products are non-food (seasonal/lifestyle). If the form has a category dropdown, choose the closest (e.g. “Seasonal” or “Non-food / Home” if available).
- **Samples:** Have samples ready to send if they request them after the form.
